Refined: Warm Light + Dark Depth

All three share the same structure — warm paper background, dark inked cards, high contrast. The accent color is what changes.

Same base. Three accents. Pick the one that feels like yours.

A — Coffee & Manuscript

Warm brown accent
Deep walnut brown for the accent. The warmest option — feels like leather bindings and espresso. Cards go full dark to create that editorial contrast you wanted. The about section uses a slightly darker warm tone to create depth without going dark.

B — Paper & Moss

Sage-green accent
Desaturated sage green pulls from palette 3 but on warm paper instead of dark base. The contrast is sharper here — cool green against warm cream creates visual tension that feels intentional, not accidental. Cards go dark slate with a slight cool undertone.

C — Kiln & Ember

Burnt sienna accent
A deeper, earthier red-brown — not terracotta (that's Claude), not orange. Think: fired clay, old brick, the warmth of a kiln. This is the boldest accent — it draws the eye hardest. Use if you want the CTA to really punch.
